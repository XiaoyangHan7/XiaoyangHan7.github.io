About Riley

Riley Han is a dedicated and innovative researcher currently pursuing his Ph.D. in Molecular Biology and Biochemistry at the School of Biological Sciences, The University of Hong Kong. His academic journey includes an M.Sc. from Imperial College London and a B.Sc. from Soochow University, where he consistently demonstrated exceptional academic performance. Riley's research interests lie at the intersection of molecular biology, CRISPR-based genome editing, and computational biology. He is driven by a passion for dissecting complex biological regulatory networks and developing novel approaches to address critical scientific challenges.

Research Experience

CRISPR-Cas System Physiology in Pseudomonas aeruginosa

Principal Investigator | 2023 - 2027

Riley is leading the investigation into the physiological regulatory functions of the CRISPR-Cas system in Pseudomonas aeruginosa. His work involves leveraging CRISPR-based genome editing and molecular cloning techniques, developing Python algorithms for gene candidate identification from high-throughput screens, and performing RNA-seq analysis to uncover epigenetic and transcriptional regulatory networks.

Development of 2A Peptide-based Gene Tagging in Trypanosomes

Master's Graduation Project | 2021 - 2023

During his Master's project, Riley successfully established a CRISPR-Cas9 system and a novel trypanosome cell line. He demonstrated the activity of T2A peptides in African Trypanosoma brucei, which is crucial for studying gene regulation, including iron homeostasis. This comprehensive project involved primer design, PCR, transfection, flow cytometry, WB, qPCR, and advanced data processing.

Anti-infection Mechanisms of Epigallocatechin Gallate (EGCG)

Undergraduate Honor Project | 2019 - 2021

As part of his undergraduate research, Riley extensively studied the anti-infective effects of tea polyphenols (EGCG) through various cell and in vivo experiments. His contributions led to a national patent and co-authorship on multiple scientific publications, including a co-first author SCI paper and a first-author Chinese SCI review.
